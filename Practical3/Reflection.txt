Completing the task for this IBI course was both challenging and rewarding. The major hurdle came when I tried to push my local changes to the remote repository. I used the command "git push origin master" but was met with authentication errors. Surprisingly, I happened to find one of my classmates who had the same problem. After looking for teachers' help, we were advised to switch to SSH authentication and started to work together to solve the problem. Fortunately, we successfully pushed the local changes to GitHub in the end.
Throughout the process, I worked with two repositories: ChiefD0766.github.io for the website and IBI1_2025-26 for course materials. Now I feel much more comfortable with basic Git commands and managing remote repositories. This task was far more than just building a website. It introduced me to the realities of version control and problem-solving in a development environment. Although the obstacles I met were frustrating at times, they forced me to dig deeper and truly understand what was happening behind the scenes. Undoubtedly, these skills will be rather useful in future projects, whether academic or professional.
